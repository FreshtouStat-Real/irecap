Improving Reading Culture for Better Academic Performance (iRECAP)

The project entitled "Improving Reading Culture for better Academic Performance (iRECAP)" is a community-based project with the aim to provide sustainable solution to poor reading culture in Oyo state. 100 students from 10 selected public secondary schools in the Akinyele Local Government Area of Oyo State, are direct beneficiaries of iRECAP project and the entire selected communities in Akinyele LGA are indirect beneficiaries of the project pilot. Beneficiaries will be trained, educated, empowered and motivated to tackle poor reading culture and improve their academic trajectory. Innovative and Equipped iRECAP library will be provided and positioned in a central location in Arikanki community, in Akinyele LGA, Ibadan, Oyo State, which will be accessible to learners from various communities in the Local Government Area. This will ignite a desire for lifelong learning which will span beyond their secondary education and encourage young people in the communities to advance to post-secondary education.

PROJECT OBJECTIVES

  1. Beneficiaries of iRECAP project will be trained and equipped with simple reading skills and resources which will ignite a desire for learning and significantly reduced the number of out-of-school children.
  2. To improve the overall academic trajectory of learners in public secondary schools in the Akinyele Local Government Area of Ibadan, Oyo state.
  3. Provide template for global action on reading culture.
  4. To boost students confidence through exposure and participation in learning activities outside the school environment, which will help maximize their overall learning experience.

KEY ACTIVITIES

  • Develop a model and approaches to teaching reading culture;
  • Distribute 2 books each to 20 students from 5 secondary schools;
  • Organize 5 book review sessions using the developed model;
  • Field trip to Kenneth Dike National Library, University of Ibadan;
  • Commemorate the World Book Reading Day (April 23rd) with a workshop on the importance of reading culture featuring authors Remi Raji, Ayo Oyeku, Yinka Egbokhare; review and reading competition;
  • Building and equipping an open, innovative iRECAP library in Arikanki community located in Akinyele LGA.

EXPECTED OUTCOMES

  • Acquisition of lifelong reading skills.
  • An improved result in the West African Examination Council (WAEC) by 60%.
  • Active participation of learners in learning activities and improved academic performances.

MONITORING AND EVALUATION

  • 100 students from selected schools in Akinyele Local Government Area of Oyo State.
  • 2 representative teacher from each selected schools.
  • Partners: Education Ministry of Oyo State, Health Ministry of Oyo State, The Booksellers Limited, Youth Ministry of Oyo State, Transport Ministry of Oyo State, Punocracy, TVO Tribe.
  • Books: "The Alchemist", "Dazzling Mirage", "Tears of the Lonely".
  • Venue: American Corner.
  • Funding: Carrington Youth Fellowship Initiative.

SUSTAINABILITY PLAN

This is essential to the long term impact the project hopes to make in the learners of the selected schools, communities and state at large. To achieve this, it is essential to partner with authors and book publishers for continuous supplies of academic resources. Furthermore, partnership and collaborations with specific Individuals (teachers, lecturers, school principal etc), non-profit organizations, Ministry of Education, Oyo State and Government will be required to ensure continuous effectiveness and sustainability of the project pilot.
